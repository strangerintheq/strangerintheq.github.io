S L / C E

by Stranger in the Q

Long-form generative artwork released by artblocks.io
and hosted immutably on Ethereum blockchain

Art Blocks allows generative artists to upload code to a smart contract. This code, written by an artist, randomly generates a work of art. When a collector creates a work of art using a smart contract, the code is run using a unique random seed. The artist cannot know this seed in advance, so the code must be written in such a way that the artist's vision matches any random input. This limitation means that Art Blocks artists must create algorithms that can create completely different high-quality images that appear to be solid.

SL/CE (slice)

This artwork explores Suprematism and generative composition, attempts to fill some gap between generated and constructed artworks. It achieves composition by slicing simple shapes into pieces by some rules and testing them against a "bad composition detector", then carefully selecting colors for shapes.

SUPREMATISM

Suprematism is an early twentieth-century art movement focused on the fundamentals of geometry (circles, squares, rectangles), painted in a limited range of colors. The term suprematism refers to an abstract art based upon "the supremacy of pure artistic feeling" rather than on visual depiction of objects.

STORY

This work is special for me; all my previous works were built around some algorithm, which was the main idea behind the work itself. I started to change it in different places in different ways, like "what happens if I do this here instead of that" and so many times and in different places.
As for this work, at first, it was also an attempt to experiment with another algorithm that came to mind, but in the end, it turned into half a year-long research, which consisted more of studying art and making decisions than directly writing code. By the way, the above-mentioned algorithm is an algorithm for slicing the shapes into smaller pieces, which gave the name to the project: Slice.
The first developments were made in mid-summer 2022; at some point, after about a month of periodic viewing of pictures produced by this algorithm - I suddenly realized that I extremely like it - minimalistic abstract combinations of shapes, which it produces are often formed into a recognizable shape, it's an interesting feeling, I wanted to enhance it.
I spent a long time looking at the works of the masters of Suprematism from the early 20th century and changing the algorithm, looking at the works again and changing the algorithm again, many many times. I was very attracted to his basic idea of not using specific images to achieve the final goal, In the process of research, I repeatedly added this or that imitation of a physical drawing, whether it was an imitation of watercolor, paper, or pencil, and then abandoned them. I increased the number of elements and decreased them.
In the end, I got a clear idea of this work as a representative of what I called for myself "computermatism" - obtaining the overall perception of an abstract image, through composition, color, size, and shape, rather than the imitation of any physical phenomenon.
It was challenging to overcome the overwhelming desire to change the code every day; it's a trap; you wake up with a new idea, you implement it, at first, you like it - and then it stops, I lost count of how many of these approaches I made, but the git all remembers.
Eventually, I finally gave up everything that resembles drawing on canvas and concentrated on composition and palette. At some point, I developed some criteria of compositions that I like and dislike, and I decided to add such an evaluative function to this work. This ended up being expressed in a "bad composition detector", in which I evaluate the areas of the figures, the percentage of their overlap, and the balance of emptiness and fullness.

SCRIPT

Each iteration starts with selecting the simplest figure; then it is placed on the canvas according to specific rules, cut along one or more "cut lines", after which the parts can be shifted or removed. The process is repeated several times for several starting figures, after which the resulting composition is obtained. Then an evaluation function comes into play, and if the composition is considered "bad", the pseudo-random composition process is repeated with partially different inputs until a "good composition" is obtained, according to this evaluation function.
In order not to forget or lose anything, at first, I saved a lot of pictures manually, but eventually, this was automated; after about a month of development, I decided enough and wrote a script, which immediately after rebuilding the typescript saved me in a separate directory 300 images from random hashes and in a separate file code that generated these images, even I'm afraid to imagine how much backlog on my laptop.

PALETTES

The choice of palettes - a very complex matter for me, some palettes, in my opinion, worked well when I used the imitation of hand drawing, and when I gave it up - stopped, others I initially really liked, and then also stopped, in the end, I wrote a special tool for selecting palettes, which immediately when you change one of the colors in the palette applies changes to the image, using this tool were made final steps in choosing palettes.

EXTRA
